Browser Wars

By Sehrish | Feb 20, 2021

Browsers are applications that allow you to utilize the internet. Some examples of browsers are:

  • Microsoft Internet Explorer
  • Navigator
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Google Chrome.

    • Browsers finally gave the general public the tools to make the WWW more accessible. Browser wars emerged when the internet first began in the late 1990s. Netscape and Microsoft were pitted against each other to see who would gain dominion over the usage of limited shared web browsers.

    In 1997 Microsoft released Internet Explorer and began shipping their operating system. It took only 4 years for Microsoft to gain 99 percent of the market. It was beginning to become a monopoly so Firefox was created as another option. As time went on, new browsers became available such as Apple and Opera and are now widely used.
