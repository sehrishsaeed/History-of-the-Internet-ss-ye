The Invention of the Internet

By Sehrish | Feb 20, 2021

The invention of the internet can be considered on par with the invention of the wheel or fire.It’s profound effect on society and contribution to globalization is something never been seen before. J. C. R. Licklider who was an American psychologist ultimately envisioned the internet as being a communication tool between people that would connect through computers. The first computers were very large and not meant to be communicative. The Sputnik scare in 1957 launched the first man-made satellite into space. This startled America and thus began the interest in more science and technology related ideas. In 1965 an MIT scientist proposed a way to send data through “packet switching”. It essentially breaks down data into blocks before sending it where it needs to go. This also helped in measuring network speeds. By 1970 many more computers began to be connected to the ARPAnet. In 1991 the internet took a different route and became a place where people could store information, and anyone could retrieve it. TThis was known as the World Wide Web. Since then, there have been many technological advances since the origins of the internet including different browser types.
